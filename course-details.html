Course Details

Comprehensive information about our Academic Programs, Departments, and Facilities

Academic Program

Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Nursing

Program Overview

The B.Sc. nursing degree program is a four-year full-time program comprising eight semesters, which prepares B.Sc. nursing graduates qualified to practice nursing and midwifery in a variety of settings.

View Full Syllabus & Details

Aims

  • Provide comprehensive nursing care knowledge.
  • Develop professional competencies.
  • Inculcate ethical values and leadership skills.

Objectives

  • Critical thinking and effective patient care.
  • Demonstrate proficiency in nursing techniques.
  • Commit to lifelong learning.

Departments and Faculty

Our academic structure and dedicated teaching staff

Departments

Mount Zion College of Nursing is organized into specialized departments to ensure focused academic delivery:

Medical Surgical Nursing

Focuses on adult health nursing and advanced clinical practice.

Child Health Nursing

Specializes in pediatric care and child development.

Obstetrics and Gynecological Nursing

Focuses on maternal and newborn health.

Mental Health Nursing

Specializes in psychiatric nursing and mental wellness.

Community Health Nursing

Focuses on public health and community care services.
